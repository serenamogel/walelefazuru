    Bella Ramsey Today: Emmy Award Nomination

    Actor Bella Ramsey received their first Emmy Award nomination for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series for their role in the HBO post-apocalyptic drama The Last of Us on July 12, 2023. They are only the second nonbinary actor to receive an Emmy nomination, and are also the fourth-youngest nominee ever for Lead Drama Actress. Ramsey said they felt “uncomfortable” submitting for a nomination in the gendered category of Best Actress and hopes there will be “more space for non-binary people to be recognized” within future categories.

    Who Is Bella Ramsey?

    Bella Ramsey is a non-binary English actor who first generated attention for their breakout role on the HBO fantasy series Game of Thrones, in which they portrayed the young noblewoman and warrior Lyanna Mormont. They are best known for their starring role as Ellie on The Last of Us, HBO’s 2023 television adaptation of the popular video game of the same name. The actor has also appeared in shows like The Worst Witch and films like Catherine Called Birdy. Their work has earned them a British Academy Children’s Award, among other nominations.

    Quick Facts

    FULL NAME: Isabella May Ramsey
    BORN: September 30, 2003
    BIRTHPLACE: Nottingham, England, United Kingdom
    ASTROLOGICAL SIGN: Libra

    Early Life and Education

    Isabella May Ramsey, better known as Bella Ramsey, was born on September 30, 2003, and grew up in Nottingham, England. Their family dabbled in the arts, with their father playing trumpet and their older sister acting in amateur groups. Ramsey began acting at age 4 and joined the Stagecoach Theatre Arts in Loughborough around age 7, studying there for about seven years. Their Stagecoach teacher described Ramsey as skilled and attentive, saying: “Bella would pick things up within two or three goes. What talent, to be able to do that.”

    Ramsey attended the King’s InterHigh online school as a child, both so they could pursue acting and because they “didn’t enjoy mainstream secondary school and wasn’t happy.” At age 10, they enrolled in the Television Workshop, a Nottingham-based drama group for young people, but says they still didn’t necessarily believe a full-time acting career would be possible. “I didn’t even consider it,” they said. “It would have blown my little brain to smithereens.”

    Game of Thrones

    Helen Sloan//HBO

    Bella Ramsey had their breakout role as Lyanna Mormont on Game of Thrones.

    Ramsey’s involvement with the Television Workshop led their to audition for Game of Thrones, which became their first credited acting role around age 11. They portrayed Lyanna Mormont, the young leader of House Mormont and an ally to Jon Snow in the HBO fantasy television series. The character is quick-witted, sharp-tongued, and unafraid to fight despite her young age. Ramsey made their debut on the show during its sixth season in 2016 and continued to appear until its eighth and final season in 2019.

    Ramsey said they were immediately drawn to the character upon reading the script, telling New York magazine: “I like how strong she—mentally, physically, personality-wise. I like how she sticks up for what she believes in, no matter the cost or the consequences.” Lyanna quickly became a fan favorite, much to Ramsey’s surprise. “I didn’t expect it at all,” they said. “I just turned up, did my filming, and would see what happens.”

    D.B. Weiss, executive producer of Game of Thrones, praised Ramsey’s performance and said casting Lyanna Mormont was particularly challenging because it meant “putting a lot of dramatic weight on the shoulders of somebody who needs to be very young.” Despite appearing on the show, Ramsey’s parents would not let them watch the violent and sexually explicit show due to their young age.

    Other Acting Roles

    In 2017, Ramsey was cast in a starring role in The Worst Witch, a CBBC television adaptation of the children’s novel series of the same name by Jill Murphy. Ramsey portrayed the protagonist Mildred Hubble, a young witch learning magic at Miss Cackle’s Academy for Witches. Ramsey said there was a feeling of sisterhood between themself and the other actors on the show, which they described as having “lots of great parts.”

    For their performance, Ramsey won the Young Performer award at the 2019 British Academy Children’s Awards. However, Ramsey departed from the series in 2020 after three seasons. They later revealed that they developed the eating disorder anorexia nervosa while filming the series and announced on Instagram that they left the show to prioritize their mental health. Ramsey said their Christian faith played a “huge part” in helping their recover from their eating disorder.

    In addition to The Worst Witch, Ramsey voiced the title character in the Netflix original series Hilda starting in 2018, portraying an adventurous young girl who moves from her enchanted forest home to a bustling city. They received a British Animation Award nomination for Best Voice Performance for the portrayal. In the show’s second season, Ramsey wrote and performed the song “The Life of Hilda” after series director Dave Peacock suggested the idea as a joke.

    Ramsey also appeared in the film Catherine Called Birdy (2022), a medieval comedy written and directed by Lena Dunham and based on a book of the same name by Karen Cushman. In the film, Ramsey plays a teenage girl living in 13th century England, attempting to avoid her father’s plans for their arranged marriage. The performance earned them a Critics’ Choice Movie Award nomination for Best Young Performer. The film also features a song called “Birdy Song,” which Ramsey wrote when they were 10, inspired by a time their friends were mean to them during a sleepover.

    The Last of Us

    Getty Images

    Pedro Pascal and Bella Ramsey co-star in The Last of Us.

    Ramsey received their highest profile role to date when they were cast in the leading role in The Last of Us, an HBO adaptation of the 2013 video game of the same name. Set in a post-apocalyptic world in which a mass fungal infection has turned most humans into zombie-like creatures, Ramsey portrays Ellie, a headstrong 14-year-old orphan who is immune to the infection. Pedro Pascal co-stars as Joel, a hardened survivor attempting to smuggle Ellie out of a quarantine zone and across a pandemic-ravaged America.

    Series co-creator Craig Mazin said dozens of young actors were considered for the role of Ellie, but he was immediately impressed by Ramsey’s audition tape, saying “It was like Ellie realized in live action. It didn’t feel like watching an actor.” Ramsey was initially reluctant to accept the role because of the fame that would come with it, fearing it would “propel me to a place I don’t want to go to in terms of being seen and being known,” but they ultimately reconsidered.

    Although Ramsey and Pascal both appeared in Game of Thrones, they didn’t share any scenes together and hadn’t met prior to working together on The Last of Us. Nevertheless, Ramsey said “that chemistry was just there immediately and just grew and developed as Joel and Ellie’s did.” Pascal and Ramsey both received wide critical praise for their performances, with the Radio Times writing that they “embody the characters tremendously, handling emotional moments and comic frissons with aplomb.”

    On July 12, 2023, Ramsey received their first Emmy Award nomination for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series for their role in The Last of Us. They only the second non-binary actor to receive a nomination (after Carl Clemons Hopkins for Hacks in 2020), and are also the fourth-youngest nominee ever for Lead Drama Actress.

    Personal Life

    Ramsey identifies as non-binary and says their gender has “always been very fluid.” As a young child, they enjoyed being mistaken for a boy by strangers. They told The New York Times in January 2023 that they “really couldn’t care less” what pronouns people used to describe them, but in July 2023, they told British Vogue “being called ‘they’ is the most truthful thing for me. That’s who I am the most.” Ramsey said they felt “uncomfortable” submitting for an Emmy nomination in the gendered category of Best Actress in 2023, and hopes there will be “more space for non-binary people to be recognized” within future categories.

    Ramsey also plays guitar and writes songs. In 2019, announced plans for a podcast called Not Bella Ramsey, which they later dismissed as “a bad idea that I had” and never released.

    • Occasionally when I look at my Instagram and see how many followers I have, it shows me how it’s going up by the minute. It blows my mind, but everyone around me keeps me grounded.
