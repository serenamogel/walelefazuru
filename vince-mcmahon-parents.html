    Promoter Roderick James “Jess” McMahon, whose parents were immigrants of Irish ancestry from County Galway, was McMahon’s paternal grandpa. Rose Davis, his paternal grandmother, was also of Irish ancestry.

    Living with his mother and stepfathers for the most of his youth, McMahon was brought up as Vinnie Lupton. Leo Lupton, one of McMahon’s stepfathers, allegedly abused his mother and attacked him when he attempted to defend her, according to McMahon.

    He graduated in 1964 from Fishburne Military School in Waynesboro, Virginia. According to reports, he has dyslexia.

    After earning his business degree from East Carolina University in 1968, McMahon entered the world of professional wrestling as a commentator for the World Wide Wrestling Federation (WWWF) for the majority of the 1970s. In 1982, McMahon bought the WWWF from his father, Vincent J. McMahon, and he nearly monopolized the previously independent US wrestling market.

    The yearly WrestleMania event, one of the most popular in professional wrestling, was created as a result of this. Before acquiring the rival business in 2001, World Championship Wrestling (WCW) was a competitor in the industry for WWE in the 1990s.

    In 2003, WWE also acquired the holdings of the departed Extreme Championship Wrestling (ECW).

    In addition, McMahon owns a professional wrestling training center and more WWE multimedia companies. In addition to wrestling, McMahon co-owned and ran the XFL twice, a football league; the second of these leagues failed after a single season owing to the COVID-19 pandemic.

    Along with co-owning the clothing line Tapout, he served as the interim president of the World Bodybuilding Federation. Since 1969, McMahon has made television appearances for WWE, initially acting as a likable play-by-play announcer.

    Since 1997, McMahon has used the character of Mr. McMahon, portraying an obnoxious, evil, swaggering tyrant who is fixated on keeping control of his business. He frequently utters the catchphrase “You’rrre firrred!”

    He occasionally participated in professional wrestling matches while adopting the persona of Mr. McMahon. He won the Royal Rumble match, the WWE Championship, and multiple pay-per-view championships.

    In response to allegations that McMahon had paid hush money over alleged affairs with former WWE employees, McMahon resigned as CEO and chairman of WWE on June 17, 2022, pending the outcome of an internal investigation. His daughter Stephanie McMahon took over as CEO and chairman.

    McMahon declared his retirement from WWE on July 22, 2022. On January 6, 2023, it was revealed that McMahon would rejoin WWE as executive chairman.
